# Document Types Reference & Drafting Templates

## DOCUMENT TYPE IDENTIFICATION

### Category 1: Pre-Contractual Instruments
- **Letter of Intent (LOI)**: Non-binding expression of interest; sets out key commercial terms for negotiation. Usually governs exclusivity and confidentiality only as binding elements.
- **Term Sheet**: Summary of key commercial terms of a proposed deal; may be binding or non-binding; common in investment, M&A, real estate transactions.
- **Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)**: Similar to LOI; common in Indian practice; often described as non-binding but frequently contains binding elements. See MOU analysis framework below.
- **Heads of Terms (HoT)**: UK/international equivalent of term sheet; common in real estate and infrastructure.

### Category 2: Investment & Corporate Instruments
- **Shareholders Agreement (SHA)**: Governs relationship between shareholders; separate from Articles of Association
- **Investment Agreement**: Governs terms of equity investment; often combined with or supplementary to SHA
- **Subscription Agreement**: Governs specific issuance and subscription of new shares
- **Convertible Note / SAFE**: Debt or quasi-debt instrument converting to equity
- **Share Purchase Agreement (SPA)**: Transfer of existing shares from seller to buyer; includes representations, warranties, indemnities
- **Business Purchase Agreement / Asset Purchase Agreement**: Transfer of business assets rather than shares
- **Joint Venture Agreement**: Governs a JV entity or contractual JV between parties

### Category 3: Commercial Contracts
- **Master Service Agreement (MSA)**: Framework agreement governing ongoing services; supplemented by Statements of Work (SOW)
- **Supply Agreement**: Long-term purchase of goods or services
- **Distribution Agreement**: Appointment of distributor; governs exclusivity, territory, pricing
- **License Agreement**: IP licensing; governs scope, territory, royalties, sublicensing rights
- **Franchise Agreement**: Franchisor-franchisee relationship; heavily regulated
- **Agency Agreement**: Principal-agent relationship; agent acts on behalf of principal

### Category 4: Real Property
- **Sale Deed**: Transfer of ownership of immovable property; must be registered in India
- **Lease Deed**: Grant of possession for a term; must be registered in India if over 12 months
- **Leave and License Agreement**: Common in India (especially Maharashtra); weaker rights than lease for licensee
- **Development Agreement**: Developer builds on landowner's land in exchange for share of developed units

### Category 5: Employment & HR
- **Employment Agreement / Offer Letter**: Terms of employment
- **Contractor Agreement / Consulting Agreement**: For independent contractors (note: classification risk especially in California)
- **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)**: Confidentiality obligations
- **Separation Agreement / Severance Agreement**: Terms of departure; often includes general release of claims

### Category 6: Government & Regulatory
- **Mining Lease**: Grant of right to mine; state government instrument in India
- **Coal Block Development & Production Agreement (CBDPA)**: Agreement between Nominated Authority (Government of India) and successful coal block bidder
- **Concession Agreement**: Government grants right to build/operate infrastructure
- **Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)**: Long-term electricity purchase between generator and buyer (often government utility)

### Category 7: Finance & Security
- **Loan Agreement**: Terms of debt financing
- **Guarantee**: Personal or corporate guarantee of another's obligations
- **Pledge / Hypothecation Agreement**: Security creation over movable assets or shares
- **Mortgage Deed**: Security creation over immovable property
- **Deed of Debenture**: Creation of debentures

## MOU ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

Every MOU review must answer these five questions before anything else:

**1. Is this actually binding?**
Look at the language throughout. "Shall" and "must" = binding. "Intends to" and "proposes to" = non-binding. Also check: has consideration passed (money paid)? If yes, that portion is binding regardless of the label.

**2. What specific clauses are binding within a "non-binding" MOU?**
Confidentiality, exclusivity, governing law, and dispute resolution are almost always binding even when the MOU says it is non-binding. Identify these and treat them as a contract within a contract.

**3. Does any term depend on future agreement?**
"Terms to be mutually agreed" = void under Indian Contract Act Section 29. "To be determined by independent valuer" = valid. Identify every such term and flag which are enforceable and which are not.

**4. What is the intended next step?**
MOU should bridge to SHA / Investment Agreement / definitive agreements. What milestones trigger that transition? Are timelines defined? What happens if they are missed?

**5. What is the exit mechanism?**
How can each party exit if the deal does not proceed to definitive agreements? Are there penalties for walking away? Is the money paid refundable or not?

## SHA / INVESTMENT AGREEMENT FRAMEWORK

### Essential Commercial Terms to Nail Down First (Before Drafting)
- Pre-money valuation (how much is the company worth before the investment?)
- Investment amount and in how many tranches?
- Resulting shareholding structure (cap table post-investment)
- Liquidation preference — what does investor get first in a sale/winding-up?
- Anti-dilution protection — what protection against future down rounds?
- Board composition — how many seats, who nominates, what is the quorum?
- Reserved matters — what decisions need investor consent?
- Exit timeline and mechanism — IPO, strategic sale, buyback?
- Information rights — what does investor get to see and when?

### Red Flags in Investment Agreements
- No liquidation preference defined = investor ranks pari passu with founders in a sale (founder-friendly, investor-unfriendly)
- No anti-dilution = investor gets diluted with no protection in future rounds
- No reserved matters = company can take major decisions (new debt, asset sale, related party transactions) without investor consent
- No information rights = investor flying blind after money is in
- No exit mechanism or vague exit = investor's money potentially locked forever
- Buyback price formula missing = price dispute on exit is guaranteed
- SHA not reflected in AoA (India) = SHA provisions unenforceable against third parties

## EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT CHECKLIST (India + California)

### India
- Clear designation and reporting structure
- Compensation: basic salary, HRA, special allowance, variable pay — structure carefully for PF and tax efficiency
- Gratuity: mandatory after 5 years continuous service under Gratuity Act
- Leave: annual, sick, casual — as per applicable state Shops & Establishments Act
- Notice period: serving notice vs. payment in lieu; garden leave provisions
- Confidentiality: what is protected, for how long after employment
- Non-solicitation: clients and employees; 6-12 months is typically enforceable
- Non-compete: essentially unenforceable post-employment under Section 27; do not rely on it
- IP ownership: all work product created during employment belongs to employer — include explicit assignment
- Moonlighting prohibition: explicitly prohibit other employment during working hours
- Social security: PF, ESI, gratuity — employer obligations
- Termination: grounds for termination with and without notice; misconduct procedure
- Applicable law and jurisdiction

### California
- At-will employment statement (California default — either party can terminate at any time for any reason with limited exceptions)
- Compensation: base salary, bonus (if discretionary, say so explicitly to avoid triggering expectation), equity (vesting schedule, cliff, acceleration)
- Benefits: health, 401k, etc.
- Non-compete: DO NOT INCLUDE — void under § 16600
- Confidentiality and trade secrets: enforceable; use CUTSA (California Uniform Trade Secrets Act) language
- IP assignment: explicit assignment of all work product; include "work made for hire" language AND an assignment as backup; note: California Labor Code § 2870 protects employee inventions made outside work time with no company resources — acknowledge this carve-out
- Arbitration agreement: optional but common; must be mutual; cannot require employee to bear costs beyond what they would bear in litigation
- Applicable law: California; jurisdiction: county where employee works
- Required California disclosures: itemized wage statement requirements, pay stub requirements, etc.

## NDA DRAFTING TEMPLATE STRUCTURE

**For Indian NDA**:
1. Parties (full legal names, CIN, addresses)
2. Background / Purpose (what is the purpose of sharing information)
3. Definitions:
   - "Confidential Information" — broad definition + specific categories + exclusions (public domain, independently developed, lawfully received from third party, required by law to disclose)
   - "Permitted Purpose" — specifically state why information is being shared
   - "Representatives" — who can the receiving party share with (need-to-know only)
4. Obligations of Receiving Party:
   - Use only for Permitted Purpose
   - Protect with at least same care as own confidential information (and no less than reasonable care)
   - Disclose only to Representatives on need-to-know basis
   - Ensure Representatives are bound by equivalent confidentiality
5. Permitted Disclosure (legal requirement to disclose — notify disclosing party first where possible)
6. Return / Destruction — on request or on termination of purpose
7. No License — sharing information does not grant any right to use it beyond Permitted Purpose
8. Term — duration of confidentiality obligations; trade secrets: indefinitely
9. Remedies — irreparable harm acknowledged; injunctive relief available without proof of damages
10. No Warranty — information shared "as is"; disclosing party does not warrant accuracy
11. Governing Law — laws of India; jurisdiction — [city]
12. Dispute Resolution
13. Entire Agreement / Amendment

**Mutual vs One-Way**: If both parties sharing → use mutual NDA. If only one party sharing → one-way NDA from that party's perspective.

## MINING INVESTMENT DOCUMENT CHECKLIST (India-specific)

Before any investment in an Indian mining company, these documents must be reviewed:
- [ ] MMDR Act allocation order / CBDPA — full review of all 13-19 termination triggers
- [ ] Mining Lease (if granted) or status of Mining Lease application
- [ ] Environmental Clearance (if obtained) or timeline/status
- [ ] Forest Clearance Stage 1 and Stage 2 (if applicable) — is the mine area forest land?
- [ ] PESA applicability — is the mine in a Scheduled (tribal) Area? Gram Sabha consent status?
- [ ] Performance Bank Guarantee — current status, not been appropriated?
- [ ] Geological Report (GR) — if a Partially Explored Mine, what is the exploration status and reserve estimate?
- [ ] Mining Plan — in-principle approval or final approval?
- [ ] Change of control restrictions in CBDPA — does the proposed investment trigger Lock-in Period restrictions?
- [ ] FDI compliance — is the investor a foreign entity? FEMA compliance required; border-country restrictions?
- [ ] Valuation — shares cannot be issued below fair value to foreign investors
- [ ] Company financials — audited accounts, net worth certificate
- [ ] Litigation search — any pending proceedings against company or directors?
- [ ] MCA filings — current and complete?

## COMMERCIAL AGREEMENT DRAFTING: KEY PROTECTIVE CLAUSES

### Liability Cap (always include)
"Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, the total aggregate liability of [Party] under or in connection with this Agreement, whether arising in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty or otherwise, shall not exceed [amount / X times fees paid in preceding 12 months]. Nothing in this clause shall limit [Party]'s liability for [fraud / death / personal injury / wilful misconduct]."

### Consequential Loss Exclusion (always include, in capitals)
"IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, PUNITIVE OR EXEMPLARY DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS OF DATA, LOSS OF GOODWILL OR BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, HOWSOEVER CAUSED AND WHETHER ARISING IN CONTRACT, TORT, STATUTE OR OTHERWISE, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES."

### Termination for Convenience (include for client)
"Either Party may terminate this Agreement for any reason or no reason by providing [30/60/90] days' written notice to the other Party. In the event of termination for convenience, [Client] shall pay [Service Provider] for all work satisfactorily completed up to the date of termination."

### Termination for Cause with Cure Period
"Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within [30] days of receiving written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice in the event of [insolvency / fraud / material misrepresentation]."

### Force Majeure
"Neither Party shall be liable for any delay or failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement to the extent such delay or failure is caused by circumstances beyond the reasonable control of such Party ('Force Majeure Event'), including but not limited to acts of God, natural disasters, pandemic, war, terrorism, civil unrest, governmental action, fire, flood, earthquake, or failure of third-party utilities. The Party affected shall: (i) notify the other Party within [5] Business Days of becoming aware of the Force Majeure Event; (ii) use reasonable efforts to mitigate the effects; and (iii) resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable. If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [90] days, either Party may terminate this Agreement by [30] days' written notice, provided that payment obligations for work already performed shall survive such termination."

### Representations and Warranties (from counterparty)
"[Counterparty] represents and warrants to [Client] that: (a) it is duly incorporated and validly existing under the laws of [jurisdiction]; (b) it has full power and authority to enter into and perform its obligations under this Agreement; (c) this Agreement has been duly authorized, executed and delivered by [Counterparty] and constitutes a valid and binding obligation enforceable against [Counterparty]; (d) the execution and performance of this Agreement does not violate any applicable law or any agreement to which [Counterparty] is a party; (e) there is no pending or threatened litigation, arbitration or regulatory proceeding which could materially affect [Counterparty]'s ability to perform its obligations hereunder."
