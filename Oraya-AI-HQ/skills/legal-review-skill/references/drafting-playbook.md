# Drafting Playbook — Protective Clause Standards

## How to Use This File
When drafting any clause from scratch or redlining an existing clause, use the templates and principles below as your starting point. Every template has: (1) the protective version, (2) the fallback position if the protective version is rejected, and (3) the absolute minimum below which you should not go.

---

## Core Drafting Principles

**Principle 1 — Specificity kills disputes**: Every obligation must be specific enough that a court or arbitrator can determine whether it was performed without needing to hear expert evidence on industry practice. "Best efforts" invites litigation. "Deliver X document by Y date" does not.

**Principle 2 — Every obligation needs a consequence**: An obligation without a consequence is a recommendation. If the counterparty fails to do X, what happens? Specify it. Remedies available: termination, liquidated damages, right to perform at other party's cost, suspension of own obligations, specific performance.

**Principle 3 — Symmetric obligations unless commercially justified**: If you are imposing an obligation on the counterparty, ask whether you are willing to have the same obligation imposed on you. If not, there needs to be a commercial justification. Asymmetric obligations are easy to spot and easy to challenge in negotiation.

**Principle 4 — Money needs three specifications**: Amount (how much), trigger (when it is due), and consequence of non-payment (interest rate, termination right, security enforcement).

**Principle 5 — Definitions govern scope**: Never use a defined term before defining it. Never use a term in the definition section that is itself undefined. The definition section is where deals are won and lost — vague definitions are the single most exploited source of disputes.

## Standard Protective Clauses

### 1. Termination Rights — Protective Version

**For the investor / weaker party**:
```
The Investor shall have the right to terminate this Agreement by written notice to the 
Company in any of the following circumstances ("Investor Termination Events"):

(a) the Mining Lease / Government Approval / [specified approval] is not granted to 
    the Company within [30/36] months from the date of this Agreement;
(b) the CBDPA / Principal Agreement is terminated, cancelled, or surrendered for any 
    reason, whether or not attributable to the Company;
(c) the Geological Report / DPR reveals coal reserves of less than [X] million tonnes 
    of commercially recoverable coal;
(d) the Company is the subject of insolvency proceedings under the Insolvency and 
    Bankruptcy Code, 2016 or any equivalent legislation;
(e) the Company materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to 
    remedy such breach within [30] Business Days of written notice from the Investor 
    specifying the breach; or
(f) the Company misapplies or misappropriates any funds contributed by the Investor,
    as determined by the Joint Auditor.

Upon occurrence of any Investor Termination Event, the Company shall refund all 
amounts paid by the Investor, together with simple interest at [12/15]% per annum 
from the date of payment, within [30] Business Days of receipt of the Investor's 
termination notice.
```

**Fallback position**: Keep (a), (b), (d), and (e) — these are non-negotiable minimums. (c) is negotiable on threshold. (f) can be softened to "independently verified" misapplication.

**Absolute minimum**: (a) and (b) must survive. If the government approvals don't come or the principal contract is cancelled, the investor must get their money back. There is no commercial justification for the investor forfeiting money in these circumstances.

### 2. Escrow Clause — Protective Version

```
All amounts paid by the Investor pursuant to this Agreement shall be held in an escrow 
account ("Escrow Account") with [Bank Name], a scheduled commercial bank, pending 
utilisation in accordance with the approved budget.

The Escrow Account shall be operated jointly by the Investor and the Company, requiring 
the signatures of [one authorised representative of each party] for any withdrawal 
exceeding INR [5,00,000].

Withdrawals from the Escrow Account shall only be made for the purposes specified in 
the Utilisation Plan attached as Schedule [X], and shall be supported by invoices or 
other documentary evidence. The Company shall provide the Investor with a monthly 
statement of all withdrawals within [7] Business Days of the end of each calendar month.

In the event of any Investor Termination Event, the balance standing in the Escrow 
Account at the date of termination shall be refunded to the Investor within [15] 
Business Days.
```

**Fallback**: If joint signatories are rejected, accept: Investor gets bank statements within 7 days, has audit rights over all withdrawals, and any withdrawal above INR [X] requires prior written notice to Investor.

**Absolute minimum**: Money must go into an escrow or designated account — not the company's general operations account. Investor must have audit rights over withdrawals.

### 3. Regulatory Responsibility Clause

**Protective version (for investor)**:
```
The Company shall be solely responsible for obtaining, maintaining, and renewing all 
Governmental Approvals required for the development and operation of the Mine, including 
without limitation the Mining Lease, Environmental Clearance, Forest Clearance, and Mine 
Opening Permission ("Regulatory Approvals"). The Investor's obligation is limited to 
funding the costs associated with obtaining Regulatory Approvals in accordance with the 
approved budget.

The Company represents and warrants that it has the legal capacity and standing to apply 
for and obtain all Regulatory Approvals required for the Mine, and that no Regulatory 
Approval is required to be obtained by any party other than the Company.

Failure by the Company to obtain any Regulatory Approval within the timeline specified 
in Schedule [X] shall constitute a Company Default, and the Investor shall be entitled 
to exercise its rights under Clause [Termination] without penalty.
```

**Fallback**: If the other side insists on investor "support" for regulatory approvals, limit this to: providing required documentation, funding costs, and attending meetings as requested — but never "sole responsibility" and never linking money forfeiture to regulatory outcomes.

**Absolute minimum**: The word "solely" must never appear in relation to investor responsibility for regulatory approvals that legally rest with the company.

### 4. Investment Cap Clause

```
The Investor's maximum total financial obligation under this Agreement and all 
agreements contemplated herein, including the Shareholders Agreement and any 
subsequent funding agreements, shall not exceed INR [X] Crores ("Maximum Investment 
Commitment"). No additional capital call, funding request, or dilution mechanism 
shall require the Investor to invest amounts in excess of the Maximum Investment 
Commitment without the Investor's prior written consent, which may be withheld in 
the Investor's absolute discretion.

For the avoidance of doubt, if the total project cost exceeds the Maximum Investment 
Commitment, the Company shall be responsible for sourcing additional funding from 
debt or other equity investors without affecting the Investor's shareholding 
percentage, provided the Investor's Maximum Investment Commitment has been paid in full.
```

**Fallback**: If a hard cap is rejected, insist on: (a) any increase beyond initial commitment requires investor's written consent; (b) investor can choose not to contribute additional amounts without losing existing shareholding.

### 5. Jurisdiction and Dispute Resolution — India

**Protective version (for weaker/Delhi-based party)**:
```
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, 
including any question regarding its existence, validity, breach, or termination, 
shall be first referred to senior management of both parties for resolution within 
[30] Business Days of written notice of dispute.

If not resolved within [30] Business Days, the dispute shall be referred to binding 
arbitration administered by the Delhi International Arbitration Centre (DIAC) in 
accordance with its Arbitration Rules. The seat and venue of arbitration shall be 
New Delhi. The arbitral tribunal shall consist of three (3) arbitrators, one 
appointed by each party, and the third appointed by the two party-appointed 
arbitrators. The arbitration shall be conducted in the English language.

The arbitral award shall be final and binding. Each party irrevocably waives any 
objection to the jurisdiction of the courts at New Delhi for the purposes of 
Sections 9, 27, 34, and 36 of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.
```

**Fallback**: If Delhi is rejected, accept Mumbai. Never accept a city where only one party is located unless that party is your client. Never accept a sole arbitrator appointed by the other party.

### 6. Limitation of Liability Clause

**Balanced version**:
```
(a) Neither party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, consequential, 
    special, punitive, or exemplary damages arising out of or in connection with 
    this Agreement, including but not limited to loss of profits, loss of revenue, 
    loss of data, or loss of business opportunity, whether or not such losses were 
    foreseeable or the party had been advised of their possibility.

(b) The total aggregate liability of either party to the other in respect of all 
    claims arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed 
    [INR X Crores / USD X] ("Liability Cap").

(c) The limitations in (a) and (b) shall not apply to:
    (i)  fraud or wilful misconduct;
    (ii) death or personal injury caused by negligence;
    (iii) indemnity obligations for third-party IP infringement claims; or
    (iv) breach of confidentiality obligations.
```

**Note for India**: Civil Code (India) equivalent protection — courts will not enforce clauses exempting liability for fraud (Indian Contract Act Section 23 on unlawful consideration; Section 65 on restitution after void contract). Always carve out fraud.

**Note for California**: California Civil Code Section 1668 voids any contract provision that attempts to exempt a party from fraud or wilful injury. The carve-out is not just good practice — it is legally required for the limitation clause to be enforceable.

### 7. Representations and Warranties — Due Diligence Style

**For investor receiving warranties from company**:
```
The Company represents and warrants to the Investor as of the date of this Agreement 
and as of the Closing Date:

(a) Organisation: The Company is duly incorporated, validly existing, and in good 
    standing under the laws of India. It has all requisite corporate power and 
    authority to carry on its business as currently conducted.

(b) Authorisation: The execution, delivery, and performance of this Agreement have 
    been duly authorised by all required corporate action. This Agreement constitutes 
    the legal, valid, and binding obligation of the Company, enforceable against it 
    in accordance with its terms.

(c) No Conflicts: The execution of this Agreement does not: (i) violate any provision 
    of the Company's constitutional documents; (ii) violate any applicable law or 
    regulation; or (iii) breach any material agreement to which the Company is a party.

(d) Government Approvals: The Company holds valid [Allocation Order / CBDPA / Mining 
    Lease] as described in Schedule [X]. The [Allocation Order / CBDPA] is in full 
    force and effect and has not been suspended, revoked, or cancelled. There are no 
    pending proceedings that would result in revocation or cancellation.

(e) No Litigation: There are no pending or, to the Company's knowledge, threatened 
    actions, claims, or proceedings against the Company that would materially affect 
    its ability to perform its obligations under this Agreement.

(f) Financial Statements: The financial statements provided to the Investor are true, 
    complete, and accurate in all material respects and have been prepared in 
    accordance with Indian GAAP.

(g) Title: The Company has [full legal right / valid mining rights] in respect of 
    the Mine. The Mine is free and clear of all encumbrances except as disclosed 
    in writing to the Investor prior to the date of this Agreement.
```

### 8. Confidentiality — Mutual Protective Version

```
(a) Each party ("Receiving Party") undertakes to keep confidential all Confidential 
    Information received from the other party ("Disclosing Party") and not to disclose 
    such Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent 
    of the Disclosing Party, except as permitted under (b) below.

(b) The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its directors, 
    officers, employees, advisors, and financing sources who have a need to know 
    such information for the purposes of this Agreement, provided such persons are 
    bound by obligations of confidentiality no less stringent than those in this 
    clause.

(c) The obligations in (a) shall not apply to information that: (i) is or becomes 
    publicly available other than through breach of this clause; (ii) was already 
    known to the Receiving Party at the time of disclosure without restriction; 
    (iii) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the 
    Confidential Information; or (iv) is required to be disclosed by applicable law 
    or court order (in which case the Receiving Party shall notify the Disclosing 
    Party promptly and cooperate to seek confidential treatment).

(d) The obligations in this clause shall survive termination of this Agreement for 
    a period of [3/5] years.

(e) The parties acknowledge that breach of this clause would cause irreparable harm 
    for which monetary damages would be inadequate, and each party shall be entitled 
    to seek injunctive relief without proof of actual damage.
```

### 9. Force Majeure — Protective Version (India — Mining Context)

```
(a) A party shall not be liable for failure or delay in performing its obligations 
    under this Agreement to the extent caused by a Force Majeure Event, provided 
    it: (i) notifies the other party within [30] Business Days of becoming aware 
    of the Force Majeure Event; and (ii) uses reasonable endeavours to mitigate 
    the effects of the Force Majeure Event.

(b) "Force Majeure Event" means any event beyond a party's reasonable control, 
    including: acts of God, natural disasters, epidemic or pandemic, war, terrorism, 
    riots, strikes (not involving that party's own employees), and the following 
    events specifically listed as Force Majeure for this Agreement: [list specific 
    events relevant to the project].

(c) For the avoidance of doubt, the following shall NOT constitute Force Majeure Events:
    (i)  ordinary commercial or financial risk;
    (ii) changes in commodity prices or market conditions;
    (iii) delays in obtaining Governmental Approvals unless caused by factors entirely 
          outside the applying party's control and despite best efforts to obtain 
          such approvals;
    (iv) failure of the other party to perform its obligations.

(d) If a Force Majeure Event continues for more than [6] consecutive months, either 
    party may terminate this Agreement by written notice, in which case the 
    Investor shall be entitled to a pro-rata refund of amounts paid but not 
    yet utilised for project purposes as certified by the Joint Auditor.
```

**Note**: In Indian mining contracts, delayed government approvals are frequently invoked as force majeure. Resist this — it allows the company to delay indefinitely while holding investor money. Sub-clause (c)(iii) above is the key protective language.

## Negotiation Psychology — How to Win Without Killing the Deal

**Lead with the obvious**: Start with jurisdiction and compliance responsibility — these are objectively wrong and hard to defend. Win these first, then move to escrow and termination rights.

**Bundle concessions**: Offer to soften the termination timelines (e.g., 36 months instead of 24 months) in exchange for the escrow structure. This creates a trade, not a confrontation.

**Reframe, don't reject**: Instead of "we won't accept this clause," say "we understand the intent here, let's achieve the same commercial outcome with language that works for both parties." Then propose your version.

**Know your BATNA**: What is your client's best alternative to this deal? If they have other options, they negotiate from strength. If this is their only option, they need to move carefully but still must protect the non-negotiables.

**Use the law as a shield**: "Our lawyers have advised that this clause conflicts with mandatory requirements under [Indian Contract Act / FEMA / California law]" is harder to argue with than "we don't like this clause."

**Never give a unilateral concession**: Every time you soften a position, ask for something in return. This preserves perceived value and maintains negotiating leverage.
