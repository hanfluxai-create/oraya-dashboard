# Phone-Only Demo Script · v1 · Apr 18, 2026

**Format:** Live phone call. 5–7 minutes. No email, no screen share, no deck.
**Why phone-only:** Both founders' numbers serve as admin. Prospects hear the product instead of reading a deck. Flash a read-only workflow account only if asked — never volunteered.
**Two variants:** P2 · Teresa (insurance lead gen) · P1 · Mazzio (automotive voice).

---

## VARIANT A — Teresa · P2 Insurance Lead Gen

**Caller:** Frankie (warm — existing relationship)
**Length target:** 6 min
**Goal of call:** Get a yes to one of three commitments — (1) she supplies leads, (2) she tests the retainer, (3) she introduces one agent from her override book.

### Opening · 30 sec

> "Teresa — thanks for making time. You closed that policy mid-call last week while we were still in our intro, which is exactly why I wanted to build this with you. I'm going to keep this to six minutes. Three minutes on what we've built, three on how it pays you. Fair?"

Wait for yes. Don't proceed without it.

### Build · 90 sec

> "We've got a voice AI that picks up every inbound insurance lead inside 15 seconds — any hour. It qualifies them on intent, coverage type, and urgency, drops them into GoHighLevel, and books a live handoff to the agent. That's it. One human signal out of the noise."

Optional if she asks "how is this different from a chatbot":

> "Chatbots read. This talks. And it talks like a human, not like press-1-for-sales. We've tested it end-to-end — it closes the qualification loop without anyone noticing it's a bot."

### Pay-you math · 90 sec

> "Two ways we can work with you. One: you send us your raw leads, we qualify, and you pay $25 to $50 per handoff — only for the ones that pick up and match your bar. Zero for the ones that don't. Two: $1,000 a week retainer, we pipe you qualified leads from our acquisition side, you close them. Which of those sounds like the shape of your day?"

Stop. Let her pick. Don't offer a third option.

### Close · 60 sec

Listen first. Then, based on her answer:

**If she picks per-lead ($25–$50):** "Great. I'll send you a one-page SOW — no back-and-forth, I just need you to sign and we route the first batch Monday. Deal?"

**If she picks retainer ($1K/wk):** "Even better. I want to start you on a two-week trial at half-weight — $500, full lead volume, no commitment past week two. If week two beats what you'd normally pay for leads, we go full. Deal?"

**If she hedges:** "Totally fair. Can you introduce me to one agent from your override book this week? I'll run the same play with them, you get the override, and you see it work before you commit. Deal?"

### Close-the-call · 30 sec

> "Last thing — SOW hits your inbox tonight. I've blocked 15 min on Monday to walk it through if you want. If not, just sign and send back. Thanks for the time, T. Talk Monday."

Hang up. Do not oversell.

### Rules of engagement

- **Never** pitch in text. Text is for locking the meeting only.
- **Never** quote a price higher than $50/lead on first call. If asked about volume pricing, defer: "Let me send you tiered pricing in the SOW."
- **Never** promise lead volume — promise lead *quality*.
- **If voicemail:** 20-second message, Frankie's voice. "Teresa, it's Frankie. Want to get you six minutes on the voice qualifier and a retainer option I think you'll like. Try me back whenever."

## VARIANT B — Mazzio · P1 Automotive Voice

**Caller:** Frankie first (has the card)
**Length target:** 5 min (Mazzio is a sales manager — he respects brevity)
**Goal of call:** Get Mazzio on a 10-minute phone demo of the inventory agent the following week. Not a pilot yes. Just the demo yes.

**PREREQ:** Inventory bug must be CLOSED before this call happens. If R13 is still open, push the call.

### Opening · 20 sec

> "Chris — Frankie at Oraya. Your card made it to me. Thirty seconds, then I'm out of your way: we built a voice agent that picks up inbound inventory calls 24/7 and knows every VIN on your lot. I want to give you ten minutes next week to hear it answer a live query. Good?"

Wait. If yes, jump to scheduling. If he asks for more, one more paragraph only.

### Build · 60 sec (only if asked)

> "It runs off your inventory feed — refreshes every four hours, twenty-plus fields per vehicle. A customer calls at 9 PM, the agent handles the conversation, pulls the exact stock number, price, trim, and books them in for a test drive. Nothing routes to voicemail. That's the whole thing."

### Demo ask · 30 sec

> "Next week — Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday — you pick. I'll call you, you put me on speaker, and I'll have the agent answer live. Ten minutes. If it sounds wrong, we hang up and you've lost ten. If it sounds right, we talk pilot terms."

Lock the slot. Then hang up.

### If he says "send me something first"

> "I'll send a one-pager — but I'd rather you hear it. Any ten minutes this week or next works. Paper is cheap. The voice is the thing."

Push once. If he still wants paper, send the one-pager (CTO-Claude ships the one-pager as Deliverable #3 in this same pack — pending). Always circle back to "when can I call you with the agent live."

### Voicemail script · 20 sec

> "Chris — Frankie Alicea at Oraya. A mutual contact passed along your card. We built a voice agent for dealer inbound — I'd love to give you ten minutes next week and have it answer a live query. I'll try you again tomorrow, or text me back at your speed. Appreciate it."

### Rules of engagement

- **Never** mention AutoNation MSA on first call. Mercedes SB first, AutoNation is the move-six.
- **Never** share the Vercel dashboard link with Mazzio — it's a working doc, not a sell sheet.
- **Never** quote pilot price on the phone. "Let's see it run, then we scope." Pricing lands in the SOW after the demo.
- **If R13 reopens mid-week:** call Mazzio back to reschedule. Do not demo a broken agent.

## Demo-day checklist (both variants)

Before either call:

- [ ] Both founders' phones charged, silenced notifications, good signal.
- [ ] Retell agent dashboard open in a second tab (monitoring, not screen-sharing).
- [ ] For Mazzio: confirm inventory bug R13 = CLOSED in the last hour.
- [ ] For Teresa: SOW draft (per-lead + retainer) ready to send within 2 hours of the call.
- [ ] Timer set for target length. If you hit the target, close. Do not overrun.
- [ ] One founder leads, the other listens muted. Swap roles per variant.

## After every call

Within 15 min:

- Log the call outcome in the dashboard's "tasks" tab as a one-liner.
- If it was a yes, start the SOW/contract clock.
- If it was a no or hedge, write one sentence: *what will change by next call*.
- Tell CTO-Claude in this thread so the brief reflects the result tomorrow morning.
