## Why we walk in

Mazzio's card was captured Apr 18 after Frankie's drop-in. The appt-setter was demo-stable as of Apr 20 PM (500+ calls tested, Teresa demo ran cleanly). The product is no longer a gating factor. The only live variable is Mazzio's timing and willingness to take a 10-minute meeting.

Week 3 silence on a handshake-warm lead means one of three things: (1) he's travelling, (2) he lost the card, (3) he forgot. All three are fixed by a low-pressure in-person ping, not by another voicemail.

## Walk-in rules

- **Dress:** clean shirt, no tie. Dealer-lot enterprise-casual. Not "pitching." Looking like a person who already works with Mercedes.
- **Arrival time:** between 2 and 4 PT. Not first-thing (too busy with morning ops), not evening (close-out). Mid-afternoon foot traffic is low; the GM is reachable.
- **Car:** park on the street or a side lot, not a customer spot.
- **Do not:** mention voicemails. Do not say "I've been trying to reach you."

## The three lines

Walk in, smile at the front-desk associate:

> "Hi, I'm Frankie from Oraya. Chris Mazzio gave me his card a couple weeks ago and asked me to swing by when I had the AI qualifier ready for him to see. Would he have 10 minutes now, or should I come back another time?"

That's it. Three sentences. You are a vendor with a scheduled-ish reason, not a cold walk-in.

### Path 1 · Chris is available

He comes out. Warm handshake. Say:

> "Chris, good to see you. I didn't want to keep leaving voicemails, figured it was faster to show you. The AI appointment-setter is live. I can run it against your actual lot in ten minutes if you have a phone on you. If not, say when and I'll come back."

If he says yes: pull up the Retell web test page on your phone, put in his number, hit call. He picks up, hears Jessica, answers two qualifier questions, and lands an invite in his own inbox. End-to-end demo in three minutes.

If he says "not now, but come back Monday": schedule it with him verbally right there. "Monday at 2 works. I'll bring the SOW too, so if you like it we can sign same day."

### Path 2 · Chris is not available

Associate says he's busy / travelling / not in.

> "No problem. Could you pass him a note? Just let him know Frankie stopped by. I'll text him this evening with a 20-minute slot offer for Monday or Tuesday. Appreciate it."

Leave a business card. Do not push for a callback time, do not ask to speak to anyone else.

### Path 3 · Associate is defensive

Rare, but possible if the dealer has a "no solicitors" policy.

> "Understood, my fault for dropping in. I'll just text him. Thanks for your time."

Back out gracefully. Text Chris from the car: "Chris, Frankie from Oraya. Swung by the dealership; you were busy. I've got the AI qualifier live and tested on 500+ calls. 10 min on your schedule this week? I can bring lunch if that helps."

## What NOT to do

- Do not demo on a customer's phone or at a sales desk.
- Do not mention Teresa, the Tuesday email, or any other Mercedes market. Keep it 1-to-1.
- Do not drop pricing numbers unprompted. The pilot is $0 / 30d / 24h exit / 72h purge. Only mention if he asks.
- Do not mention AutoNation escalation unless he asks about multi-rooftop. If he does: "That's conversation number four or five. Let's make sure you love it on your lot first."
- Do not hand over the SOW PDF unless the meeting lands on-site. Keep it in the car.

- Path 2 / 3 result: left card + what was promised for follow-up

If Path 1 and the demo ran, push a dashboard update with `Mazzio · demo delivered in-person Apr 22` under Intelligence Feed, and queue the pilot SOW for same-day email. If Path 2 or 3, leave Mazzio at "warm, week 3" and plan one final outreach by Apr 28 before recasting as dormant.
