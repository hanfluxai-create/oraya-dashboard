## Operating principle

"Remove all the noise, only look for the signal." (Mtg 23 resolve, post cousin passing.) The owner is the signal. Champions are noise if they cannot return an email in 72 hours.

The walk is not a pitch. It is a 90-second qualifying interaction designed to either (a) get a same-day or same-week 30-min sit-down with the owner, or (b) get a definitive "no, here is who handles this" so we stop wasting time on this dealership.

---

## Pre-walk prep (the night before)

- Print 5 leave-behinds (folded one-pager, see below)
- Bring 5 blank business cards as backup
- Suit, polished shoes, no laptop bag (looks consultative-y, makes receptionists suspicious)
- Phone fully charged with the Retell live-demo number on speed dial
- Bring iPad with the demo dashboard pre-loaded and offline (no wifi gamble inside the dealership)
- Walk into the showroom at 10:30 AM PT (post-morning-meeting, pre-lunch-rush). Avoid Saturdays.

## 30-second elevator (memorize verbatim)

> "Hi, I'm Frankie Alicea with Oraya Labs. I sent your team a pilot proposal Friday for an AI voice agent that handles after-hours sales calls and inventory questions. I happened to be down from the Bay so I figured I'd stop in instead of waiting on email. Would the GM or owner have 5 minutes today, or should I leave a one-pager for whoever is right for this conversation?"

That is it. No "do you struggle with after-hours calls." No "we save you money." No demo offer. The ask is binary: 5 minutes today, or the right name.

## Receptionist gate (most likely first contact)

**Receptionist response possibilities and Frankie's branch:**

- "Let me get someone." → Wait calmly. Have leave-behind ready.
- "They are busy, can I take a message?" → "Of course. Could I leave this one-pager and grab the GM's direct email so I can follow up Monday by name?" Then leave the leave-behind. Do not push for the meeting twice in the same visit; that converts you into a salesperson in their head.
- "We are not interested." → "Totally fair. Just to make sure I am following up the right way: who is the right person to keep informed on this when our pilot data is ready in 30 days?" Capture name. Walk out.
- "Do you have an appointment?" → "No, I was nearby. If now is not a good time, I would love to leave a one-pager and try to come back this afternoon. Is the GM in today?" Capture yes/no.

**Hard rule:** do not ask the receptionist to evaluate the product. Their job is the front desk, not vendor selection. Do not pitch. Do not demo. Do not show the iPad.

## If you get the GM (Mark Akbar) face to face

You have 5 minutes. Use them in this order:

1. **30-second framing:** "I run a voice AI company. We help dealerships catch sales calls and inventory questions after hours and on weekends without paying a service. I sent a pilot proposal Friday. I was in town and figured a face is better than an email."

2. **One question:** "What percentage of your inbound sales calls go to voicemail in a typical week? Take a guess, ballpark." (Whatever number he says, validate it: "yeah, the dealerships we have looked at run 25-40 percent. We can recover most of that.")

3. **One demo prop:** Hand him the iPad. Pull up the live Retell demo. Let him talk to the agent for 60 seconds. Do not narrate. Let the product talk.

4. **One ask:** "I would love 30 minutes with you and whoever owns the customer-experience side. Pilot is 30 days, zero platform fee, written results report at Day 30. Can we put something on the calendar Monday or Tuesday?"

5. **Exit:** Whatever he says, give him a leave-behind and a card, and leave within 60 seconds of the close. Do not loiter. If he passes, follow up by email Monday with a thank-you and a calendar link. Do not push.
