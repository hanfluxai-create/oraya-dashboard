Why Compliance Is Not Optional: It Is Our Foundation

Every voice call to or from a California consumer triggers a matrix of federal and state obligations: TCPA prior express written consent (PEWC) for outbound automated calls (47 U.S.C. §227(b); 47 C.F.R. §64.1200), CIPA all-party recording disclosure (Cal. Penal Code §§ 632, 632.7), CPRA data-handling and consumer-rights obligations, FTC bot-and-deceptive-practice rules under §5 of the FTC Act, and California's Bolstering Online Transparency Act (SB 1001, Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code §§ 17940-17943) requiring clear identification of automated agents. A single violation can expose a dealership to statutory damages of $500 to $1,500 per call under the TCPA and $5,000 per violation under CIPA. Most AI vendors treat compliance as an afterthought; we treat it as architecture.

Our six-vendor stack (Twilio, RetellAI, n8n Cloud, Supabase, Google Vertex AI, and a Vercel-hosted dashboard) is built on providers that maintain SOC 2 Type II attestations or equivalent enterprise security certifications; current attestation reports are available under NDA. For outbound automated calls, we enforce PEWC verification before dialing; for inbound calls, we deliver the CIPA all-party recording disclosure and the SB 1001 automated-agent identification at the start of every interaction. We maintain an immutable consent ledger with row-level access controls and apply data-minimization and retention rules consistent with the CPRA. Our full Post-Implementation Legal & Technical Compliance Verification Report, prepared by Oraya in-house compliance counsel, is available on request for review by your counsel and IT leadership.

Post-Pilot Scenarios

We believe in earning your business through demonstrated performance. At the conclusion of the 30-day pilot, there are three possible outcomes:

Scenario 1: Results meet expectations.  We transition to a production agreement at competitive monthly pricing that is transparent, rooftop-based, and designed for ROI from Day 1. A detailed proposal will be presented at the Day 30 review.

How We Measure Success

The Day 30 report will quantify performance across four dimensions: operational efficiency (booking rate, handle time, first-call resolution); revenue impact (appointments booked, RO re-engagement conversions, recovered missed-call revenue); customer experience (satisfaction indicators, transfer rate, escalation frequency); and compliance posture (consent verification rate, disclosure delivery, data-purge logs, and a written compliance-posture report based on available logs identifying any known issues and remediation steps).
