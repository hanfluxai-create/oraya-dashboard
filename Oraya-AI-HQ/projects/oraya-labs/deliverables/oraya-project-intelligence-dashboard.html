Phase 4: Regulatory Deep Dive (7 Statutory Areas)

1. TCPA / FCC-24-17: AI Voice Classification

The Core Rule: FCC Order 24-17 (February 8, 2024) formally classified AI-generated voices as "artificial or prerecorded" under the TCPA. This means ALL outbound AI voice calls require prior express written consent (PEWC) before dialing, regardless of the content or purpose.

The Vacatur: The FCC's separate "one-to-one consent" rule (requiring seller-specific consent for each caller) was vacated by the Eleventh Circuit on January 24, 2025, the day before it was set to take effect. The Insurance Marketing Coalition challenged it successfully. This means broad lead-generator consent remains valid for now.

Oraya's Posture: Despite the vacatur, our system enforces the most conservative possible standard: caller-specific PEWC verification on every single outbound call via Supabase consent_log query before RetellAI initiates the dial. This means even if the one-to-one rule is reinstated, we are already compliant. This is the compliance-as-architecture principle.

Statutory Exposure: $500 per violation (negligent), $1,500 per violation (willful/knowing). No cap. Class action exposure is significant for any dealer making volume outbound calls.

2. CIPA §§ 632 / 632.7: California All-Party Consent

The Core Rule: California Penal Code § 632 prohibits recording confidential communications without consent of ALL parties. § 632.7 extends this to cellular and cordless communications specifically. Smith v. LoanMe (2021) confirmed that automated calls ARE covered.

Oraya's Implementation: Hard-coded recording disclosure at the start of every call (inbound and outbound). The consent disclosure script includes: AI identity, recording notification, purpose statement, and opt-out mechanism. Non-consenting callers trigger the basic_attributes_only purge via webhook to RetellAI, destroying audio and transcript within the API call itself.

Statutory Exposure: $5,000 per violation under § 637.2. Criminal liability possible under § 632 (imprisonment up to one year).

3. CPRA / CCPA: Data Minimization & Service Provider

The Core Framework: CPRA (effective Jan 1, 2023; regulations finalized Sept 22, 2025, effective Jan 1, 2026) requires data minimization, purpose limitation, and proper service provider agreements. Oraya AI processes dealership customer data ONLY as a "service provider" under § 1798.140(ag).

Oraya's Architecture: Data processing agreement (DPA) language prepared per CPRA § 1798.100(d). Oraya does NOT sell, share, or use personal information for any purpose other than the contracted service. Supabase consent_log with RLS ensures data cannot be accessed by unauthorized roles. Retention policy: 30-day auto-purge for non-consented data.

Open Gap: DPAs must be formally executed (countersigned) with ALL six vendors. Language is drafted but not yet legally binding.

4. CPPA ADMT: Automated Decision-Making Technology

The Core Rule: California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) finalized ADMT regulations in September 2025, effective January 1, 2026 (limited), with full compliance required by April 1, 2027. ADMT applies to technology that processes personal information to make or facilitate "significant decisions."

The Gray Zone Analysis: Customer service scheduling (booking test drives, service appointments) is NOT a "significant decision." The regulation targets employment scheduling, credit decisions, insurance underwriting, and housing. AI deciding to escalate to a human is a routing decision, not a significant decision. AI classifying a lead as "appointment booked" vs. "callback requested" is data categorization. These do NOT trigger ADMT obligations.

Oraya's Posture: System is architecturally exempt by restricting to scheduling and Q&A functions only. No financing, credit, or insurance decisioning occurs in the voice pipeline. Document this exemption clearly in all compliance materials. If scope ever expands to include automated credit pre-qualification or insurance quoting, ADMT will be triggered and full compliance (opt-out mechanism, pre-use notice, access to logic) will be required by April 1, 2027.

5. FTC AI Bot Identity / Operation AI Comply

The Core Rule: FTC's Operation AI Comply (September 2024) specifically targets companies that use AI to deceive consumers. The FTC's position: any AI system that interacts with consumers must clearly disclose its artificial nature at the outset of the interaction.

Oraya's Implementation: Hard-coded system prompt includes AI identity disclosure within the first 10 seconds of every call: "This is Jessica, an AI assistant calling on behalf of [Dealership Name]." This is not configurable by the dealership, not skippable by the agent, and is logged in every call transcript.

6. SB 446: California Breach Notification

The Core Rule: SB 446 (effective January 1, 2026) tightens California's breach notification timelines: 30 days to notify affected consumers, 15 days to notify the Attorney General (if 500+ California residents affected).

Oraya's Incident Response: Documented IR plan with SB 446-aligned timelines: (1) Identification & containment within 4 hours, (2) Executive notification within 12 hours, (3) Consumer notice within 30 days, (4) AG notice within 15 days. Tabletop exercise recommended before pilot launch to validate response times.

7. TISAX / NP.50.14.110: Mercedes OEM Security Mandate

The Core Requirement: Mercedes-Benz requires all contractors and technology vendors working with dealership data to comply with TISAX (Trusted Information Security Assessment Exchange). NP.50.14.110 is the Mercedes-Benz norm that mandates ISMS implementation.

Timeline: September 30, 2026 deadline (per Mercedes-Benz dealership communications). This is a hard deadline that applies to all dealership technology vendors.

Oraya's Position: Vercel (our dashboard host) holds TISAX Level 2 certification. This is a genuine differentiator: no competitor in the automotive AI voice space publicly claims TISAX-certified infrastructure. Our vendor certification bundle (SOC 2 across all layers + TISAX on the dashboard) positions Oraya as the vendor that HELPS dealerships prepare for OEM security audits, not one that adds risk.
