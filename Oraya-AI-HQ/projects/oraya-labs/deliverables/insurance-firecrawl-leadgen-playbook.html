Life Insurance Lead Engine · Channel-First, Consumer-Second

Firecrawl is the wrong tool to chase consumers and the right tool to recruit the people who already touch consumers daily. This playbook builds a 30-partner referral network across 7 license states inside 90 days, then layers a TCPA-safe consumer funnel underneath. Target output: 140 qualified leads/mo, 35 policies/mo, $67K/mo gross commission by Day 90.

License footprint: CA · TX · ID · NV · NC · SC · GA · LA Carriers: all majors · 110% advance, 140% at $60-100K/mo issued Voice agent: Maya (Retell + Claude Haiku 4.5) Stack: Firecrawl + n8n + Supabase + Smartlead + Maya

Strategic Thesis

Why Firecrawling consumer life-insurance prospects is a trap

There is no public consumer email database on the open web. "Top life insurance buyers in zip 90404" returns content marketing pages, not contact lists. Anyone selling that list bought it from a data broker who scraped opt-in form leakage; sending to it nukes domain reputation in 5-7 days. Consumer cold-email volume above 1K/day with sub-90% deliverability is a one-way ticket to Spamhaus.

Why Firecrawl wins on B2B partner acquisition

Mortgage brokers, CPAs, estate attorneys, fee-only RIAs, realtors, HR benefits brokers, SBA lenders publish their contact info by design on professional directories, association sites, and firm pages. Firecrawl extracts it cleanly. One signed referral partner = 3-7 warm leads/mo for 24+ months. The unit economics dominate consumer cold by 10x.

B2B partner LTV

5 leads/mo × 12 mo × 25% close × $1,920 commission = $28,800/yr per partner. Add $1K/wk retainers from top-tier producers. Average partner LTV across 3 yrs: ~$60K. Payback on acquisition: under 30 days.

Consumer cold email LTV

0.1% reply × 5% qualified × 8% close = 4 policies per 100K sends. $7,680 gross at $40K acquisition cost (warmup, infra, list cleaning, domain churn). Negative ROI for first 9 months. Reputational scorched earth on the way.

Hybrid play (recommended)

Firecrawl = partner discovery engine. Partners = warm-lead source. Maya = qualification + booking. Frankie + sub-agents = close. Stripe captures commission split + retainer. This is what the vision file already calls "10-15 high-producer agency owners × $1K/wk." Firecrawl is the missing intake.

Why this fits Oraya's actual moat

Frankie's superpower is relationship capital with agency owners. The bottleneck on scaling that capital is discovery — finding the next 50 owners worth pursuing. Firecrawl industrializes discovery. Maya handles the qualification work the partner does not want to do. The system multiplies Frankie, it doesn't replace him. That is the right shape for our two-person co at this stage.

End-to-End Pipeline Architecture

The flow, top to bottom

1

Firecrawl crawl jobs (scheduled, per source)

n8n cron triggers Firecrawl per directory + per state. Webhook returns extracted JSON to /webhook/firecrawl-ingest. Avg crawl: 2-6 hrs per source, 200-1500 records, $30-90 in credits.

2

n8n normalization → Supabase staging table

partners_raw table: id, source, state, name, firm, email, phone, city, license_number, specialty, raw_payload, scraped_at. Dedup by (firm + state + name) + (email) + (phone). Cuts ~30% noise.

3

Email enrichment (Apollo + Hunter fallback)

For records with no email: Apollo.io API by name + firm domain ($0.02/lookup). Hunter.io as fallback ($0.01). Recovers ~40% of missing-email rows. Also returns LinkedIn URL + role title for personalization.

4

Email validation (NeverBounce)

Bulk validate $0.004/email. Categorize: valid, invalid, accept-all, disposable, unknown. Promote only valid + accept-all to partners_clean. Drops bounce rate from ~25% to under 4%.

5

Segmentation + scoring

Score = (state_priority × license_weight × firm_size × specialty_match). Top quartile → A-list (Frankie sends from his inbox). Middle → B-list (Maya-as-SDR sequences). Bottom → C-list (newsletter only).

6

Smartlead.ai cold sequences

10 warmed inboxes across 3 sender domains (orayalabs.ai · oraya-partners.com · frankiealicea.com). 4-touch sequence over 14 days. Personalization tokens: {first_name}, {firm}, {specialty_hook}, {license_state}. Send velocity: 30/day per inbox week 1, ramp to 50/day week 4.

7

Reply triage → GHL CRM

Smartlead webhook → n8n → Claude Haiku classifier (interested · question · objection · unsubscribe · OOO · referral) → routed: interested books Calendly, question gets templated answer, objection routes to Frankie inbox, unsubscribe purges from sequence within 24 hrs.

8

Maya 15-min discovery call

Calendly booking triggers Retell call window. Maya runs partnership-discovery script (clientele, average policy size appetite, current carrier appointments, referral comp expectations). Post-call summary → GHL deal record + Frankie Slack ping if hot.

9

Frankie close → partnership agreement

Standard agreement: Oraya quotes + binds, partner gets 30% of first-year commission split, partner promoted to Oraya's "Trusted Advisor" co-brand for credibility. E-sign via Stripe-linked DocuSeal. Auto-onboarding email with referral form + tracking link.

10

Lead intake from partners → Maya consumer flow

Partner submits referral via tracked form → consumer gets opt-in SMS + email → consumer clicks → Maya consumer call → quote → Frankie or licensed sub-agent closes → carrier e-app via RPA → policy bound → Stripe captures commission split → partner paid + tracked in dashboard.

Compliance Posture · Read This Before Sending One Email

The legal frame

B2B cold email to professional contacts at their work address for a commercial purpose is legal under CAN-SPAM (15 USC §7701) without prior consent, provided we satisfy five rules. TCPA (47 USC §227) does not apply to email — it covers calls, SMS, and fax. GDPR does not apply (US-only targets, US-based sender). State exceptions are minor and listed below.

CAN-SPAM five rules · all five must be present in every send

  1. Accurate header information. "From" name = real sender (Frankie Alicea or Maya at Oraya Labs). Reply-to = monitored inbox. No spoofing.
  2. Non-deceptive subject line. Must reflect content. "Re:" only if there is a real prior thread.
  3. Identification as advertisement (this is satisfied implicitly when commercial intent is clear in the body — no separate label required for B2B).
  4. Valid physical postal address in every email footer. Use Oraya Corp's registered California address (or PO Box if preferred). Update across all 10 inboxes.
  5. Functional opt-out mechanism processed within 10 business days. Smartlead's native unsubscribe handles this; n8n cleanup job verifies removal within 24 hrs.

State-level addenda

  • California (SB 568): If the email is not personalized to an individual recipient, prepend subject line with "ADV:" or include "Advertisement" notice. Personalized emails (which all of ours are) are exempt.
  • Maryland MCEMA + Utah Restricted Email Registry: Both deprecated by federal preemption. Safe to ignore.
  • NAIC + state insurance regs: Frankie's solicitation must include license # in any email that pitches insurance products to a consumer. B2B partner outreach is NOT a consumer solicitation — license # not required at this layer. Add only when consumer-facing.

Domain + sender hygiene (the part that actually matters)

  1. SPF, DKIM, DMARC set to p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@orayalabs.ai on all 3 sender domains. Already done on orayalabs.ai (Apr 22 · Mr. Jenkins). Replicate on the 2 secondary domains.
  2. Domain warmup 14 days minimum before scaled send. Smartlead native warmup pool handles this.
  3. Send velocity caps per inbox: 30/day week 1, 40/day week 2, 50/day week 3+. Above 50/day per inbox is where Google starts flagging.
  4. Bounce rate ceiling 3%, spam complaint ceiling 0.1%. If any inbox crosses, pause that inbox, audit list, resume after 7 days.
  5. Never use the orayalabs.ai primary domain for cold volume. Frankie's signature domain stays clean for replies, demos, signed partner comms. Cold goes through secondary domains exclusively.

Hard "do not do" list

  • Do not scrape and mail consumer email lists (residential, generic gmail/yahoo, opt-in-form leakage). Burns reputation, attracts FTC.
  • Do not send to anyone whose email indicates "info@", "contact@", "admin@", "noreply@" without dedup. Generic inboxes have higher spam-trap density.
  • Do not embed pixel trackers in cold emails. Apple Mail Privacy Protection makes them useless and they trigger spam filters.
  • Do not send any cold email pitching life insurance to the consumer until they have self-initiated contact via opt-in landing page. The whole TCPA-safe consumer thesis depends on user-initiated conversation.

Consumer Layer · Build After Partner Pipeline Is Live

Why this comes second, not first

Consumer acquisition is more expensive and more fragile than B2B partner acquisition. Build it once partners are flowing leads, because partner-warm leads (a) prove Maya works, (b) generate the case-study data we need for ad creative, and (c) fund the consumer experiment without burning runway.

The TCPA-safe consumer funnel (per existing P2 design)

  1. Paid acquisition · Meta + Google PMax targeting (a) new homeowners, (b) parents 30-50, (c) HNW small biz owners. Geo-fence to 7 license states only.
  2. Landing page · orayalabs.ai/insurance (already queued in dashboard) with the embedded Maya web voice widget · user-initiated click = TCPA-safe.
  3. Maya consumer call · 4-7 min discovery, captures coverage need + budget + health profile.
  4. Quote engine · Pulls from carrier APIs (or RPA where APIs don't exist) — returns 3 best-fit policies.
  5. Calendly with Frankie or sub-agent for binding call.
  6. Carrier e-app via RPA (Puppeteer pattern) — on the Quote AI roadmap.
  7. Stripe captures commission split + records consent trail for compliance.

Consumer data sources (NOT Firecrawl)

  • Atom Property API · already named in P2 spec · new homeowner records.
  • Versium / FullContact / Acxiom · enrichment from anonymized identifier.
  • CMS Medicare Public Use Files · for the 65+ vertical.
  • Voter rolls (state-by-state) · age + household basics where legal.
  • Meta Custom Audiences from CRM · lookalike off our converted policies.

None of this is Firecrawl-shaped work. Firecrawl's value is unstructured-web extraction; consumer data is API + database work.

Why this layered approach matters strategically

We become the only insurance lead-gen co that has both: (a) a B2B partner moat that throws off cash and refers warm leads at high close rate, and (b) a TCPA-safe consumer engine that generates the policy volume needed to unlock 140% carrier commissions. Most lead-gen shops have (b) only and live and die on Meta CPMs. We have (a) as the floor.
